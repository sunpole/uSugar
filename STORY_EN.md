# The Magomedov Family Story and the Making of uSugarBot
## 🧬 uSugar
### Your sugar, your rules
### Твой сахар под контролем
*Family diabetes assistant for Libre2 · Семейный помощник при диабете*

**Minsk, Belarus. 2026.**

There are five of us: me, Anton (40), my wife Olga (turning 42 this summer), our eldest son Nikita (turned 13 in April), daughter Vera (will be 6 in August), and our youngest son Matvey (will be 9 in September).

Nikita was diagnosed with type 1 diabetes when he was one and a half years old — mid‑2014. He was our first child. We tried to give him everything we could, even if things were far from perfect. At some point, the child began to weaken noticeably. Every day he became less active, and soon he didn’t even want to leave his stroller to play at the playground. He was drinking a lot and urinating very frequently. My wife took him to the paediatrician, suspecting diabetes. But the doctor reassured her and suggested monitoring him for another week. Four days later, Nikita developed a very high fever. He just lay there without moving, but kept asking for food. My wife called the paediatrician, and we left Nikita with his grandmother and went to work.

When the doctor arrived, she said we had to call an ambulance immediately. My wife rushed home from work, and she and our son were taken to the hospital. There they ran a lot of tests and took blood. As soon as the results came back, they urgently moved him to the intensive care unit. Nikita had diabetes. His blood sugar upon arrival at the hospital was 33 mmol/L. They immediately put him on a drip. By the very next day, he felt better.

Nikita stayed in the ICU for four or five days while they treated an ear infection and stabilised his condition. Then he was transferred to the children’s endocrinology ward. There, everything became much simpler and clearer. Day by day he improved. I rarely visited. As a father, I couldn’t forgive myself for not protecting my child. My life just stopped. By then I hadn’t drunk or smoked for three years and was actively training — I couldn’t understand why this was happening.

Two weeks later, Nikita was discharged. He had changed completely. Before Nikita was born, a medical check‑up had revealed that I had hepatitis C — a consequence of my past with drugs. From 16 to 25, I was a heroin and hard‑drug addict. My son’s illness and my own diagnosis shattered me. I went on a binge for two and a half years. I drank in the morning, at lunch, and in the evening — I poured myself into oblivion. I didn’t want to live. I saw no point in continuing such a failed life. I only dreamed that my son would become a normal child, but every month he seemed to get worse. My relationship with my wife nearly fell apart. We barely spoke. I understood I couldn’t be the support they needed, and that killed me even more. I tried as hard as I could, but it was probably only a tenth of what I should have done and what was expected of me.

More than ten years have passed since then. Nikita is now thirteen. Life has changed dramatically. I found solace, and that solace saved our family, our marriage, and brought us two more wonderful children — Vera and Matvey.

*Now I want to invest my free time and modest programming knowledge into building a Telegram bot that can help my son. The bot will recognise Libre2 screenshots, calculate insulin doses, keep a diary, and send reminders. Perhaps this project will be a small compensation for everything I couldn’t give during the hardest years.*

**This bot is my attempt to do something that makes life a little easier for Nikita — and maybe it will inspire other parents facing the same struggle.**
